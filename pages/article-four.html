What Are Cells?

Have you ever wondered what makes up every living thing on Earthâ€”including you? The answer is cells. These microscopic units are the smallest structures capable of performing all the functions of life. Whether itâ€™s a blade of grass, a buzzing bee, or a human body, cells are the foundation. In fact, your body contains around 37 trillion cells (Bianconi et al., 2013)!


Cells are often referred to as the "building blocks of life", and for good reason. They form the structure of all organisms and carry out the processes needed to survive.


The Cell Theory, a fundamental concept in biology, states that:

  • All living things are made up of cells.
  • The cell is the basic unit of structure and function in living organisms.
  • All cells come from pre-existing cells.

This theory was developed in the 1800s thanks to scientists like Matthias Schleiden, Theodor Schwann, and Rudolf Virchow, who used early microscopes to study plant and animal tissues.
(National Institute of General Medical Sciences, n.d.)

Organelle Function

Organelle Function

Other Important Structures

Additional cell components include:

  • Lysosomes - Contain digestive enzymes to break down waste
  • Vacuoles - Storage sacs (especially large in plant cells)
  • Cytoskeleton - Network of fibers that maintains cell shape
  • Centrioles - Help organize cell division (animal cells only)
  • Cell Wall - Rigid outer layer (plant cells only)
